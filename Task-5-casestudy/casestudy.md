# Cybercrime Case Study (UPI / QR Code Fraud)

## Case Study: The QR Code Scam
In a recent incident involving a university student, the victim was lured into a "QR Code Scam" while attempting to sell a used textbook on an online marketplace. The scammer, posing as a buyer, sent a QR code via WhatsApp, claiming it was for "receiving the payment." Unaware that QR codes are only used for sending money and never for receiving it, the student scanned the code and entered their UPI PIN. Within seconds, ₹15,000 was debited from their account.

**Analysis**: This crime succeeded due to a lack of digital literacy regarding payment gateway mechanics. The perpetrator used psychological manipulation (social engineering) by creating a sense of urgency. The consequence was not just financial loss, but also a compromise of the student's banking credentials.

**Prevention**: To prevent such incidents, users must remember that receiving money via UPI never requires entering a PIN. Any request to "scan to receive" is a definitive red flag for fraud.
